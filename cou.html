Elon Musk (South Africa/United States)
- CEO of Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink and The Boring Company.
Net Worth: $189.0 billion

Early Life and Education (1971-present):
Born in Pretoria, South Africa in 1971, Musk displayed an aptitude for computers and coding at a young age.
He sold his first video game at 12! After moving to Canada and then the United States, he earned degrees in physics and economics from the University of Pennsylvania.
He later started a Ph.D. program in physics at Stanford but left after two days to pursue entrepreneurial ventures.

Serial Entrepreneur (1995-present):
Musk's entrepreneurial journey began with Zip2, a web software company for the media industry.
He co-founded X.com, an online financial services company, that later merged with Confinity (which had developed PayPal) to become the payment giant we know today.
In 2002, Musk set his sights on space exploration with SpaceX, aiming to reduce space transportation costs and colonize Mars.
SpaceX has achieved significant milestones, including developing reusable rockets and becoming a major player in the commercial spaceflight industry.
In 2004, he invested in Tesla Motors, an electric vehicle company, eventually becoming its CEO and leading the company's transformation into a leader in electric cars and clean energy solutions.
Musk's entrepreneurial spirit doesn't stop there.
He has founded or co-founded several other companies, including Neuralink (focusing on neurotechnology interfaces), The Boring Company (developing underground transportation systems), and OpenAI (a non-profit research company for safe artificial intelligence).

Disruptive Innovation and Public Figure (present):
Musk is known for his audacious goals and relentless pursuit of innovation.
He pushes boundaries in industries like electric vehicles, space travel, and sustainable energy.
His outspoken personality and use of social media like Twitter have made him a controversial figure, attracting both passionate supporters and harsh critics.
In 2022, he acquired Twitter (later renamed X) with the goal of promoting free speech and making the platform more transparent.
